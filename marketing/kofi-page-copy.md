## About section (the long pitch)

> ### What is DevBoard?
>
> A notes app that thinks visually. Markdown editor with `[[wikilinks]]` and backlinks, plus a real infinite canvas with sticky notes, shapes, connectors, and code blocks — in **one** workspace.
>
> The workspace is just a folder on your disk. Your notes are plain Markdown files. Your canvases are JSON. You can open the same folder in VS Code, version it with git, sync it with Syncthing, back it up however you back up files. No account. No cloud. No telemetry.
>
> Available in your browser, and as a desktop app for macOS, Windows, and Linux.
>
> ### Why I built it
>
> I was tired of keeping notes in one app and diagrams in another, and never being able to link between them. I wanted something that respected my files, didn't ask for an account, and didn't gate the whiteboard behind a "pro" tier.
>
> Most of the existing options are either US-based SaaS with a subscription, or local-first but missing the canvas. DevBoard is local-first, EU-built, and free — with the canvas built in.
>
> ### Why Ko-fi?
>
> The app is free and stays free. No pro tier, no upsell, no "free for small workspaces." If it replaces a subscription for you, or just makes your work easier, a coffee covers a couple more evenings of building. Tips also unblock features I'd otherwise have to deprioritize — better mobile, more export formats, plugin API.
>
> ### Try it
>
> - **Browser:** mfgoes.github.io/Devboard
> - **Desktop:** mfgoes.github.io/Devboard/download.html
> - **Manual:** mfgoes.github.io/Devboard/manual.html
>
> Thanks for being here. ☕
